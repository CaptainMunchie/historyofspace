The Expansion of the Universe is Speeding Up

In 1998, the scientific community had discovered that our universe is not only expanding at an accelerating rate but the opposite.

In 1998, the scientific community had discovered that our universe is not only expanding at an accelerating rate but the opposite.

They believed that the gravitational attraction by matter within the universe is slowing down the expansion. This idea has brought the birth of "dark energy", which is thought to "permeate the universe and exert a repulsive force on all large-scale structures". Its nature remains unknown but is estimated that 68% of the overall universe is made up of dark energy.

An Ocean on Jupiter's Moon

In 1995, NASA's Galileo mission has confirmed the existence of a massive ocean of liquid water, concealed beneath the icy crust of Jupiter's moon Europa.

In 1995, NASA's Galileo mission has confirmed the existence of a massive ocean of liquid water, concealed beneath the icy crust of Jupiter's moon Europa.

Images captured by the Galileo mission suggested that the ice is floating on top of an ocean estimated to be 100 miles deep and contains twice the amount of Earth's ocean.
